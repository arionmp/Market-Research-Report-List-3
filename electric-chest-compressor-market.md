<p><p>The Electric Chest Compressor market includes both invasive and non-invasive types. Invasive chest compressors require insertion into the body, typically through the mouth or nose, to provide mechanical chest compressions during CPR or resuscitation. Non-invasive chest compressors, on the other hand, deliver compressions externally through the chest wall. Each type offers unique benefits and considerations, with invasive chest compressors often providing more consistent and effective support during cardiac arrest situations, while non-invasive chest compressors may be more user-friendly and easier to use in certain settings.</p></p>
