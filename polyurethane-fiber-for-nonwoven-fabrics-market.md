<p><p>Polyurethane fibers for nonwoven fabrics are categorized based on their melt flow rate, with three main types - < 25 min, 25-40 g/10 min, and > 40 g/10 min. Fibers with a melt flow rate of < 25 min are considered low melt flow rate fibers, offering high strength and toughness. Those in the range of 25-40 g/10 min are medium melt flow rate fibers, balancing strength and flexibility. Fibers with a melt flow rate of > 40 g/10 min are high melt flow rate fibers, known for their softness and elasticity.</p></p>
