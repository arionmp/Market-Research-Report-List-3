<p><ul><li>Push–Pull Hand Controls</li><li>Push Right Angle Hand Controls</li><li>Push–Rock Hand Controls</li><li>Push–Twist Hand Controls</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive hand controls are devices that enable individuals with disabilities to operate a vehicle. Push–pull hand controls allow braking and accelerating by pushing or pulling a lever. Push right angle hand controls use a horizontal handle for both braking and accelerating. Push–rock hand controls have a rocking motion for braking and accelerating. Push–twist hand controls require twisting a handle to control the vehicle. These different types cater to the varying needs and preferences of individuals with disabilities, providing them with options for driving independently.</p></p>
