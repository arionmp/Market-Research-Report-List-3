<p><p>Solid scintillators are materials that emit light when charged particles pass through them, such as crystals or plastic scintillators. Liquid scintillators are solutions that produce light when charged particles interact with them, commonly used in detector systems for neutrino experiments. Gaseous scintillators are gases that emit light when particles ionize them, often utilized in particle detectors. Each type of scintillator material has its own unique properties and applications, playing a crucial role in various scientific and industrial sectors.</p></p>
