<p><p>Stainless steel alloys are categorized into three main types: Austenitic, Ferritic, and Martensitic. Austenitic stainless steel is the most common type, known for its high corrosion resistance and strength. Ferritic stainless steel is less corrosion resistant than austenitic, but offers good formability and magnetic properties. Martensitic stainless steel is known for its high hardness and strength, making it suitable for applications requiring wear resistance. Each type has its own unique properties and characteristics, catering to different needs in the market.</p></p>
