<p><p>Clinical immunoanalyzers are tools used in medical laboratories to detect and quantify specific molecules in bodily fluids. Common types of immunoanalyzer markets include Chemiluminescence Immunoassay (CLIA), Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA), Radioimmunoassay (RIA), Fluoroimmunoassay (FIA), and others. CLIA uses chemiluminescence reactions for detection, ELISA uses enzyme-labeled antibodies, RIA uses radioisotopes for detection, and FIA uses fluorescent labels. Each type offers different advantages and applications in the clinical setting.</p></p>
