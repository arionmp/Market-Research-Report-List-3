<p><p>The C-Reactive Protein Test (CRP) is a blood test used to measure the levels of C-reactive protein in the body, which is produced by the liver in response to inflammation. Elevated levels of CRP in the blood can indicate inflammation in the body, which may be due to a variety of causes such as infection, autoimmune disorders, or chronic diseases.</p><p>The global C-Reactive Protein Test (CRP) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.00% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, growing awareness about the importance of early detection of inflammation, and advancements in technology leading to improved accuracy and efficiency of CRP tests.</p><p>Moreover, the market is witnessing a trend towards the development of high-sensitivity CRP tests, which can detect even low levels of CRP in the blood, allowing for early detection of inflammation and better management of related health conditions. Additionally, the rising adoption of point-of-care testing for CRP is expected to further drive market growth, as it offers quick results and convenience for both patients and healthcare providers.</p></p>

<p><p>C-Reactive Protein Test (CRP) Market includes various types such as ELISA, Immunoturbidimetric, CLIA, and others. ELISA, or enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay, is a common method for detecting CRP levels in the blood. Immunoturbidimetric assay measures the amount of CRP based on the degree of turbidity. CLIA, or chemiluminescence immunoassay, is another method used for quantifying CRP levels. Other types of CRP tests may include immunofluorescence assays or nephelometric methods, offering a range of options for healthcare providers.</p></p>
